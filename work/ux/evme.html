Introduction

Electronic Vehicle(EV) is key to reducing pollution/emission that contributes to climate change and smog caused by conventional vehicles. My initial thought was to get a better way to find the charging station near all EV owners. The mobile app for finding the nearest EV charging station will make potential EV buyers more convinced to purchase, and eventually benefit the companies who are selling EV like Tesla, General Motors, and Nissan and more over contribute to a cleaner environment.

Problem

Not every gas station in the United States has an EV charger, and inconvenience of finding EV charging stations would directly affect the potential EV buyer to hesitate on purchasing.

    Project Plan

    The entire project was planned within the 8 weeks scope from July 12, 2021 to August 22, 2021. I created gantt chart using google spreadsheet’s plug in called “Slick Gantt” and set it up with five different phases. Each phase got subtasks belong to it and tried to spread out the tasks within the 8 weeks time frame. This project plan plays an essential role in helping guide stakeholders, sponsors, teams, and project managers through other project phases. Planning helped to define the goals, reduce the risks and make sure deliver the product by the due dates.

    Design Process

    After planning out the idea story board, I started to narrow down the scope of project in detail and gather data and research to see if this idea is feasible and demanded. First of all, I wanted to know if we could pull the data from EV to app so that app can calculate the price and charging time automatically based on user’s car information. I found there are many APIs in the market which provides estimated charging time, battery level, capacity, and charging status. I tried to expend the idea further by creating visual map, placed main idea at the center, then brainstorm around the idea using data and research. I also added assumptions and basic user flow ideas while in the early stage.
